From the Desk of Pastor Curtis

From the Desk of Pastor Curtis

Dear Sisters and Brothers in Christ,

As we celebrate Mother's Day this month, I want to highlight a crucial aspect of our Kingdom work: equipping the next generation of Kingdom citizens. As we honor the mothers in our lives, let's remember that Kingdom mothers are not just caregivers, but agents of transformation, shaping the hearts and minds of the next generation to reflect the King's values and character.

In Matthew 19:13-15, Jesus welcomes children to Himself, declaring that the Kingdom belongs to them. He doesn't say, "The Kingdom belongs to the grown-ups." No, the King sees children as vital components of His Kingdom, deserving of love, care, and spiritual nourishment. As Kingdom mothers, we have the privilege of instilling in our children a deep understanding of their identity as beloved children of God and co-heirs with Christ.

As we raise the next generation, let's intentionally pass on the values of the Kingdom. Let's teach our children to love the King's Word, to trust in His goodness, and to live as agents of transformation in their own spheres of influence. As Proverbs 22:6 says, "Train up a child in the way he should go, even when he is old he will not depart from it." This is not just a parental responsibility, but a Kingdom mandate.

So, I want to challenge you, dear mothers and caregivers, to see yourself as a Kingdom mother. Ask the King for wisdom, guidance, and empowerment to shape the next generation. As you do, remember that you are not just shaping individuals, but influencing the future of the Kingdom. Let's rise to this noble calling, knowing that the King is with us, empowering us to transform the world around us.

May the King bless you richly as you serve Him in this vital role.

Pastor Curtis

Kingdom Intelligence

Christians Show Resilience Amid Persecution in Ethiopia

In a small village on Ethiopia's western border with Sudan, 18-year-old Zamzam Addaamaa found herself in a desperate fight for survival after choosing Jesus as her faith. Haroji Wado, a predominantly Muslim region, has become increasingly hostile towards Christians, forcing Zamzam and many others to flee their homes. Despite these dangers, Zamzam remains committed to her faith, and her story is a testament to the resilience of Christians facing persecution worldwide.

Kingdom Perspective: As Christians face increasing persecution, we must remember that our loyalty to Jesus is not tied to earthly comfort, but to His lordship over all things, empowering us to stand firm in the face of adversity.

Christians Unite to Feed Displaced in Lebanon

In Lebanon, a Christian migrant community is providing vital support to those displaced by war and economic hardship. Grace, a migrant domestic worker from Kenya, has found a steady job and is now feeding hundreds of refugees through her local church. This remarkable act of love and service is a powerful expression of the Kingdom's presence in the world, where Christians are called to care for the vulnerable and bring hope to those in need.

Kingdom Perspective: As the Kingdom advances globally, we see Christians demonstrating the love of Jesus through acts of service and compassion, reflecting the lordship of Christ over all spheres of society, including education, business, and social welfare.

Christians Unleash Prayer Movements Across the Globe

In an era of increasing darkness, Christians around the world are responding with bold prayer movements, seeking to bring the light of Jesus to their communities. From spontaneous prayer gatherings in cities to large-scale prayer events, believers are rising up to intercede for their nations and the world. This surge in prayer is a declaration of Jesus' lordship over all things, calling forth His presence and power to transform lives and cultures.

Kingdom Perspective: As the people of God unite in prayer, we are reminded that the Kingdom is not just a future hope, but a present reality, where Jesus is Lord over all, and His people are empowered to bring heaven to earth through prayer and obedience.

Kingdom Living

Kingdom Living: Embracing the Legacy of Kingdom Mothers

As we celebrate the theme of Kingdom Mothers this month, we're reminded that our mothers, grandmothers, and female role models have passed down a rich heritage of faith, love, and Kingdom values. This month, we invite you to tap into this legacy and live out the values of Kingdom mothers in your own life.

Practical Action Steps:

  1. Nurture Kingdom Culture at Home: Take time to intentionally teach and model Kingdom values to your children or younger family members. Use everyday moments like mealtime, bedtime stories, or car rides to share stories of God's goodness and Jesus' lordship. Ground your conversations in scripture, using verses like Proverbs 22:6, "Train up a child in the way he should go; even when he is old he will not depart from it."
  2. Influence at Work: Reflect on how you can use your workplace as a platform to announce Jesus' lordship. Look for opportunities to serve, pray, or share your faith with coworkers in a non-confrontational way. Consider starting a prayer group or Bible study in your office to create a Kingdom culture among your colleagues.
  3. Build Kingdom Relationships: Identify one person in your life who may be struggling or feeling disconnected from God's love. Take the initiative to reach out, listen, and share the hope of the Kingdom with them. This might be a friend, family member, or neighbor.
  4. Engage in Community Service: Join a local community organization or volunteer group that aligns with the values of Kingdom mothers. Serve others in need, using your skills and talents to make a difference in your community.

As you live out these practical action steps, remember that you are part of a larger Kingdom family. Celebrate the legacy of Kingdom mothers in your life, and be empowered to pass on this heritage to those around you. Let's rise up as Kingdom citizens, influencing our world with the love, kindness, and generosity of our Heavenly Father.

Scripture of the Month

Scripture of the Month

Matthew 12:46-50 (NIV)

"But he said to them, 'A prophet is not without honor except in his own hometown and in his own household.' And he did not do many mighty works there because of their unbelief. Then he went around teaching from village to village, preaching the good news of the kingdom and healing those who had various diseases, and a great many people were following him. And he saw a tax collector named Levi sitting at the tax booth. 'Follow me,' he said to him. And Levi got up and followed him. And leaving everything, he got up and followed him."*

The Kingdom of God is often seen as a future hope, but in this passage, Jesus demonstrates its present reality. While his own family and hometown refuse to acknowledge his authority, Jesus is still preaching the good news of the kingdom and performing mighty works. This is a powerful example of the Kingdom's counter-cultural nature, where those who are often marginalized or oppressed become the focal point of Jesus' ministry.

This passage is especially relevant to the theme of Kingdom Mothers, as it highlights the role of women like Levi's mother in supporting and following Jesus. The passage also underscores the call to discipleship, where we are invited to leave behind our old lives and follow Jesus into a new reality.

Meditation: What would it mean for you to leave behind your old life and follow Jesus into a new reality of Kingdom living?
