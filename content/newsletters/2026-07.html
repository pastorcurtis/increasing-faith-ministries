From the Desk of Pastor Curtis

Dear beloved family,

As we step into the month of July, I want to remind you that our theme, Kingdom Freedom, is not just a phrase, but a living reality that breaks into our lives every day. As we walk in the light of the King, we experience true freedom - freedom from the chains of sin, freedom from the fear of the world, and freedom to live fully alive in the Kingdom.

Jesus declared, "If the Son sets you free, you will be free indeed" (John 8:36). This freedom is not just a feeling or a circumstance, but a reality that is rooted in the Person of Jesus Christ. As we submit to His reign in our lives, we experience the freedom to be who He created us to be, to live out our purpose, and to fulfill the good works He has prepared for us (Ephesians 2:10).

But Kingdom freedom is not just a personal experience; it's also a call to live out our lives in such a way that we reflect the reign of Christ in every sphere of society. We are ambassadors of the Kingdom, called to bring light and life to the dark and dying world around us. As we live out our freedom, we become a source of hope, healing, and transformation to those around us.

So, I encourage you to live out your Kingdom freedom with purpose and intention. Use your gifts, talents, and resources to advance the Kingdom. Engage in the culture with the mind of Christ, speaking truth, love, and light to those who need it most. And as you do, remember that you are not just living for yourself, but for the glory of the King who sets you free.

May you walk in the freedom of the Kingdom, living fully alive in the light of the King.

Pastor Curtis

Hindu Nationalists Back Off: Pastor Continues to Share Gospel

In a recent development in India, Pastor Manish, who has been sharing the gospel full-time in local Hindu communities, has seen Hindu nationalists push back against his ministry. Despite the backlash, Pastor Manish continues to lead worship services and preach the gospel to those who are open to hearing it. The recent attack on his Sunday worship service has only strengthened his resolve to share the good news of Jesus Christ. Pastor Manish's unwavering commitment to Jesus' lordship is a testament to the power of the gospel to transform lives.

Kingdom Perspective: As believers in Jesus, we are called to be salt and light in the midst of spiritual darkness, even in the face of opposition, just as Jesus instructed His followers to be witnesses to the ends of the earth.

Congo Pastors Pray and Fight Misinformation Amid Ebola Outbreak

In the midst of the ongoing Ebola outbreak in the Democratic Republic of Congo, Pastor Denis Dalanga and his network of churches are not only providing aid to those affected but also praying for healing and fighting misinformation that has spread fear and panic. By standing firm in the midst of crisis, Congo pastors are demonstrating the power of faith in Jesus to overcome even the most daunting challenges. Their resilience and commitment to the gospel is a beacon of hope in a world ravaged by disease and despair.

Kingdom Perspective: As Jesus' lordship advances in every sphere of society, we see His followers responding to crisis with compassion, prayer, and the courage to confront misinformation, exemplifying the Kingdom's transformative power in the midst of chaos.

Persecuted Christians in Indonesia Receive Support from Global Community

In a heartening display of solidarity, Christians around the world have rallied to support persecuted believers in Indonesia after a prayer house was attacked by a violent mob. The attack, which came in response to a group of Muslims demanding that the prayer house be closed, has sparked an outpouring of support and prayers from the global Christian community. This outpouring of support is a testament to the global reach of Jesus' kingdom and the unbreakable bonds of unity that exist among His followers.

Kingdom Perspective: As we see the global community come together to support persecuted believers, we are reminded that Jesus' kingdom is not bound by national borders or denominational divides, but is a unified, global fellowship that stands in solidarity with one another in the face of adversity.

Kingdom Living

Kingdom Living: Embracing Kingdom Freedom

As we celebrate Kingdom Freedom this month, we're reminded that Jesus' lordship over all creation gives us the power to break free from the chains of sin, fear, and doubt. As Kingdom citizens, we're called to live differently in every sphere of life.

In our homes, this means creating a space where Kingdom values are lived out. Here's a practical step: Schedule regular family prayer and worship times. Set aside a specific time each day or week to pray, sing, and discuss God's Word together. This can be as simple as a 10-minute breakfast devotional or a 30-minute family worship night.

At work, Kingdom freedom means being a light in the midst of darkness. Take a Kingdom-based approach to your job by seeking opportunities to serve others. Look for ways to bless your colleagues, customers, or clients. Whether it's a kind word, a helping hand, or a thoughtful gift, your actions can be a witness to Jesus' love.

In our communities, Kingdom freedom means standing up for justice and loving our neighbors. Volunteer your time and resources to support a local ministry or initiative. Whether it's serving at a food bank, participating in a community clean-up, or advocating for a worthy cause, your actions can make a difference.

In our relationships, Kingdom freedom means living with humility and love. Practice active listening by asking questions and seeking to understand others' perspectives. This simple act can help break down barriers and build bridges.

As we live out these practical steps, remember that Kingdom freedom is not about us; it's about Jesus. "You were taught to put aside your old self (sinful, unspiritual), just as you are being made new in the image of your Creator" (Colossians 3:10, NLT). Let's live out our new identity in Christ this month!

Scripture of the Month

Scripture of the Month

John 14:23-25 (NKJV)

"Do not let your heart be troubled; you believe in God, believe also in Me. In My Father's house are many mansions; if it were not so, I would have told you. I go to prepare a place for you. And if I go and prepare a place for you, I will come again and receive you to Myself; that where I am, there you may be also."

As Jesus prepares to leave His disciples and return to the Father, He gives them a profound revelation about the Kingdom of God. The promise of a place for them in the Father's house is not just a reference to heaven after death, but a declaration of the King's provision for those who belong to Him. The "many mansions" (lit. "dwelling places") represent the diverse expressions of the Kingdom's presence and power in the world. Jesus is not just preparing a place for them in the afterlife, but also equipping them to live as Kingdom citizens in the here and now.

This passage connects to our theme of Kingdom Freedom because it highlights Jesus' authority and provision as the Lord of the Kingdom. His promise to return and receive us to Himself speaks to the present reality of the Kingdom, where we are already citizens of heaven (Philippians 3:20). As we live in the freedom of the Kingdom, we are empowered to live as ambassadors of the King, influencing every sphere of society with the love and power of God.

Meditation: How are you living as a citizen of the Kingdom in the midst of uncertainty and change?
