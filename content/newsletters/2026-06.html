From the Desk of Pastor Curtis

Dear friends in the Kingdom,

As we enter the month of June, I want to encourage you to reflect on the calling of fatherhood as a reflection of Kingdom leadership. The Bible says, "He is a father to the fatherless, a defender of widows" (Psalm 68:5). Fatherhood is not just a role, but a sacred trust that models the character of God Himself.

In the Kingdom, fathers are not just providers, but leaders who demonstrate the love, authority, and provision of our Heavenly Father. As Jesus taught, "Which of you, if your son asks for bread, will give him a stone? Or if he asks for a fish, will give him a snake?" (Matthew 7:9-10). Fathers, like God the Father, give good gifts to their children, not because they are obligated, but because they love them.

As Kingdom fathers, we are called to lead by example, teaching our children the ways of the King and modeling the values of our heavenly Father. We are to be "gentle as a nursing mother" (1 Thessalonians 2:7), yet firm and authoritative, just as God is. We are to provide for our children's physical needs, but also to nurture their spiritual growth, discipling them in the ways of the Kingdom.

This month, I encourage you to reflect on your own fatherhood, whether biological or spiritual. Ask the Lord to reveal areas where you can improve, and seek His guidance on how to model Kingdom leadership to those in your influence. Let us strive to be fathers who demonstrate the character of God, leading our children and those around us into the fullness of the Kingdom.

May the Father's blessing be upon you and your family this month. Remember, you are not just fathers - you are Kingdom leaders, called to shape the next generation of disciples who will proclaim the Kingdom and carry its influence into every sphere of society.

Pastor Curtis

Kingdom Intelligence

Global Church Growth: A Resilient Movement

In a recent survey, it was found that Christianity is growing at an alarming rate in Africa, with over 500 million Christians projected to be added to the continent's population by 2050. This growth is largely attributed to the efforts of local churches and missionaries who are planting new churches and spreading the gospel in rural areas. Despite persecution and challenges, the church in Africa continues to thrive and grow. Kingdom Perspective: As the Kingdom advances globally, it is clear that Jesus' lordship is being proclaimed and accepted in the hearts of millions of Africans, further establishing the Church as a vital part of His redemptive plan.

Advancing the Kingdom in Brazil: Christians in the Public Square

In a significant development, Christian psychologists in Brazil are pushing back against government efforts to restrict their ability to express their faith in the public sphere. After years of pressure, they have won a major victory, allowing them to practice their faith in the midst of their professional work. This move is a testament to the power of the Kingdom to shape culture and influence society. Kingdom Perspective: As the Kingdom of God advances in Brazil, it is clear that Jesus' lordship is being proclaimed in every sphere of society, including education, healthcare, and the media, further establishing the Church as a counter-cultural force for good.

Prayer Movements and Perseverance in the Face of Persecution

In India, over 180 Christian families have been denied access to water and economic rights due to their refusal to renounce their faith. Despite these challenges, the Christians in the region are choosing to persevere, relying on prayer and their faith to sustain them. This remarkable display of resilience is a testament to the power of the Kingdom to overcome even the most daunting obstacles. Kingdom Perspective: As the Kingdom advances globally, it is clear that Jesus' lordship is being proclaimed in the midst of persecution and adversity, further establishing the Church as a community of overcomers who trust in the power of God to sustain them.

Kingdom Living

Kingdom Living: Embracing the Father's Heart

As we delve into the theme of Kingdom Fathers, we're reminded that God's heart is a model for us to follow. As Kingdom citizens, we're called to embody the qualities of a loving Father – providing, protecting, and guiding those around us.

In the sphere of our homes, let's strive to create a safe and nurturing environment where our loved ones feel seen, heard, and valued. This month, take these practical steps:

  1. Schedule quality time: Set aside dedicated time for meaningful conversations with your family members. It could be a weekly dinner, a game night, or a simple walk together.
  2. Practice active listening: When engaging with your family, make a conscious effort to fully listen to their thoughts, feelings, and concerns. Give them your undivided attention.
  3. Show physical affection: Physical touch releases oxytocin, the "love hormone," which strengthens relationships. Hold hands, give hugs, and show affection in ways that feel natural to you.

In the workplace, let's strive to be a fatherly presence, guiding and empowering our colleagues. This month, take these practical steps:

  1. Mentor someone: Offer guidance, wisdom, and encouragement to someone in your workplace who could benefit from your experience.
  2. Foster a positive work environment: Create a culture of kindness, respect, and empathy in your workplace. Speak words of encouragement and celebrate each other's successes.
  3. Take ownership of a project: Step up and take the lead on a project that needs initiative. Demonstrate responsibility, accountability, and a willingness to learn.

As we go about our daily lives, let's remember the words of Ephesians 6:4, "Fathers, do not exasperate your children; instead, bring them up in the training and instruction of the Lord." May we embody the Father's heart and guide those around us with wisdom, compassion, and love.

Scripture of the Month

Luke 3:23-24 (NIV)

"In his public life he was like John the Baptist: he did not eat or drink, and they said, 'He is a demon!' The Son of Man came eating and drinking, and they say, 'Here is a glutton and a drunkard, a friend of tax collectors and sinners.'"

In this passage, Luke highlights the distinctives of Jesus' kingdom life. Unlike John the Baptist, Jesus is not a solitary figure living in the wilderness, preparing for a future kingdom. Rather, Jesus is a king who is actively engaging with the world, eating and drinking with tax collectors and sinners. This is a powerful demonstration of the present reality of the kingdom. Jesus' kingdom is not something he is waiting to establish in the future; it is already breaking into the world, and he is embodying it in his life and ministry.

As Kingdom Fathers, we are called to follow Jesus' example, being agents of God's kingdom in every sphere of life. This means that we don't live in isolation from the world but rather engage with it, loving and serving those who are far from God. We are not just waiting for the future; we are living out the present reality of the kingdom, demonstrating Jesus' lordship in every area of our lives.

Meditation: How can you be a Kingdom Father who embodies the present reality of Jesus' kingdom in your daily life, engaging with the world and living out Jesus' lordship?
