Resurrection Power: Living in the Power of the Risen Christ

Dear Friends in the Kingdom,

As we enter the month of April, I am reminded of the transformative power of the risen Christ. The resurrection of Jesus is not just a historical event, but a present reality that breaks into our world, empowering us to live as agents of Kingdom transformation. Today, I want to encourage you to tap into this Resurrection Power and experience the fullness of life in Christ.

In Acts 2:24-27, we read, "But God raised him up on the third day and showed him openly to his disciples, who were overjoyed when they saw him. As a result, he spoke of them in these terms: 'I confer on you a kingdom, just as my Father conferred one on me, so that you may eat and drink at my table in my kingdom and sit on thrones, judging the twelve tribes of Israel.' And then he said, 'I will put you on a level with me, if you renounce the world's wrong way of thinking, and learn to see things my way.'" Here, Jesus declares that His followers will participate in the Kingdom, with all its power and authority.

As we live in the power of the risen Christ, we discover that our lives are not confined to the limitations of the world. We are called to think, live, and influence like Jesus, carrying the Kingdom into every sphere of society. This means being people of hope, courage, and compassion, even in the face of challenges and uncertainties.

So, I want to encourage you to tap into the Resurrection Power of Jesus. How? By aligning your thoughts, words, and actions with the Kingdom of God. By trusting in the power of Christ to break into your world, to bring life, hope, and transformation.

As we journey through this month, I challenge you to live in the power of the risen Christ. May His Resurrection Power transform your life, and through you, impact the world around you.

May the Resurrection Power of Jesus be your strength and your joy!

Pastor Curtis

Kingdom Intelligence

Faith in Africa's Largest City

In Lagos, Nigeria, a new church has emerged with a focus on reaching the city's 20 million residents. Led by Pastor Sam Adeyemi, the church has grown rapidly, with over 40,000 attendees. The church's emphasis on social justice and community service has drawn in young professionals and families, creating a vibrant community that reflects the Kingdom of God's presence in the city. Kingdom Perspective: As Jesus' lordship advances in Lagos, a new generation of leaders is rising to proclaim the gospel and demonstrate the Kingdom's love in the midst of Africa's largest city.

Prayer Revolution in the Middle East

In Israel, a movement of intercessory prayer has been spreading across the country, with thousands gathering in Jerusalem's streets to pray for peace and spiritual awakening. The movement, led by prayer networks and churches, aims to bring revival to the region and advance the Kingdom of God's presence in the land. As believers pray for Jerusalem, they are reminded of Jesus' declaration that the city will be the epicenter of God's glory. Kingdom Perspective: As the Church in Israel prays for peace and revival, they are aligning themselves with the Kingdom's plan to bring light to the nations and demonstrate Jesus' lordship over the Middle East.

Christianity Grows in a Muslim-majority Nation

In Pakistan, the number of Christians has been increasing despite persecution and violence. According to a recent survey, there are now over 4 million Christians in the country, up from 3.5 million in 2015. This growth is attributed to the work of missionaries and local churches that have been planting communities and spreading the gospel in the midst of adversity. Kingdom Perspective: As the Kingdom advances in Pakistan, it is a testament to the power of the gospel to transform lives and bring hope to a nation often plagued by extremism and violence, demonstrating Jesus' lordship over the hearts of those who call themselves Muslim.

Kingdom Living

Kingdom Living: Resurrection Power in Every Sphere

As we celebrate the Resurrection Power of Jesus this month, we're reminded that His victory over death and sin breaks into every area of our lives. As Kingdom citizens, we're not just recipients of this power, but also agents of transformation in every sphere. Here's how you can live out Resurrection Power in your daily life:

Empower Your Home: Renew Your Routines

This month, intentionally bring the Resurrection Power into your daily routines at home. Replace negative patterns or habits with Kingdom-affirming disciplines, such as:

  • Starting each day with a 10-minute gratitude reflection
  • Creating a 'thank-you' note or small gift for a family member or roommate
  • Setting a 'resurrection hour' for prayer, fasting, or Bible reading

Influence Your Work: Live with Humility and Faith

As you navigate your work or school environment, remember that Jesus is Lord over every domain. Practice humility and faith by:

  • Asking for feedback from coworkers or peers to grow in your role
  • Taking initiative to serve others, even in small ways
  • Sharing your faith or values with a colleague or friend, using your 'Kingdom witness'

Transform Your Community: Live with Kingdom Love

In your neighborhood or community, embody the Resurrection Power by:

  • Serving at a local food bank or outreach event
  • Hosting a block party or gathering to connect with neighbors
  • Speaking words of life and encouragement to those around you

Scripture to ground your action: "If the Spirit of him who raised Jesus from the dead is living in you, he who raised Christ from the dead will also give life to your mortal bodies through his Spirit, who lives in you." (Romans 8:11, NIV)

This month, remember that Resurrection Power is not just a doctrine, but a living reality that breaks into every area of your life. Take practical steps to embody it, and watch God transform your home, work, and community through you.

Scripture of the Month

Scripture of the Month

Ephesians 1:19-22 (NIV)

"I have raised him up to sit at my right hand in the heavenly realms, far above all rule and authority, power and dominion, and every name that is invoked, not only in the present age but also in the one to come. And God placed all things under his feet and appointed him to be head over everything for the church, which is his body, the fullness of him who fills everything in every way."*

As we explore the theme of Resurrection Power, we find ourselves in the midst of Paul's majestic declaration of Jesus' lordship in Ephesians 1. The Apostle reveals that Jesus has been exalted to the right hand of God in the heavenly realms, far above every earthly power and authority. This is not a future hope, but a present reality. Jesus is not merely the King of some distant, spiritual realm; He is the Lord of all creation, with dominion over every sphere of society.

This passage reminds us that the gospel is not just about personal salvation, but about the announcement of Jesus' lordship over all things. As citizens of the Kingdom, we are called to live in light of this reality, recognizing that Jesus is not just a distant figure, but a present, reigning King who fills all things with His presence. May we be empowered by the Resurrection Power of Jesus to live as agents of Kingdom transformation in every area of life.

Meditation: How would your life change if you truly believed that Jesus is Lord over every aspect of your world, not just the "spiritual" parts?
