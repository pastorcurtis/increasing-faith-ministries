From the Desk of Pastor Curtis

Dear friends of Increasing Faith Ministries,

As we begin this new month, I am reminded of the call to advance the Kingdom of God. Our theme for March is Kingdom Advancement, and I want to encourage you to press forward with me into new territory for the King. In Luke 10:2, Jesus says, "The harvest is plentiful but the workers are few. Ask the Lord of the harvest, therefore, to send out workers into his harvest field." This verse speaks to the reality that there is much work to be done in the Kingdom, and we are called to be part of the labor force.

As we consider Kingdom Advancement, let us not forget that it is a present reality, not just a future hope. Jesus is Lord over all creation, and we are ambassadors of the Kingdom in every sphere of society (2 Corinthians 5:20). This means that our work, our families, our communities, and our governments are all to be influenced by the Kingdom of God.

To press forward into new territory for the King, we must be willing to take risks and trust in His guidance. In Isaiah 43:19, God says, "See, I am doing a new thing! Now it springs up; do you not perceive it?" He is doing a new thing, and we are called to be part of it. This means that we must be willing to step out of our comfort zones and into the unknown, trusting that the King will guide us every step of the way.

As we press forward into new territory, let us remember that it is not about us, but about the King. Our work is not to advance ourselves, but to advance the Kingdom. Let us ask the Lord of the harvest to send out workers into the harvest field, and let us be willing to be part of that labor force.

I encourage you to press forward with me into new territory for the King. Let us be bold, let us be brave, and let us trust in His guidance. Remember, you are a Kingdom citizen, and you have a part to play in advancing the Kingdom of God.

Pastor Curtis

Kingdom Intelligence

Global Church Growth: A New Era of Multiplication

A recent report from Mission Network News highlights the rapidly growing Church in Mexico, with a 20% increase in baptisms over the past year. This growth is largely attributed to the efforts of local churches and missionaries who are engaging in intentional discipleship and community outreach. In one notable example, a church in Guadalajara has seen a significant increase in attendance and baptisms since implementing a new discipleship program. The church is now planning to launch several new congregations in surrounding areas to reach more people with the Gospel.

Kingdom Perspective: As the Kingdom advances globally, we see the power of Jesus' lordship in multiplying His Church, as people from all nations are drawn to His presence and become part of His family.

Advancing the Kingdom in the Marketplace: A New Book

In a recent interview, author Laurie Krieg shared about her new book, "Raising Wise Kids in a Sexually Broken World." The book offers practical guidance for parents seeking to raise children who will be equipped to navigate the complexities of a post-Christian world. Krieg emphasizes the importance of teaching children a biblical sexual ethic that is grounded in the Gospel, rather than simply relying on cultural norms. By doing so, parents can empower their children to live out their faith in the marketplace and advance the Kingdom in their spheres of influence.

Kingdom Perspective: As Jesus' lordship over all creation is acknowledged, His followers are empowered to influence every sphere of society, including the family and the marketplace, with a Kingdom perspective that transforms culture.

Persevering in the Face of Persecution: A Testimony from India

A recent report from International Christian Concern shares the tragic story of a Christian family in India who were killed by their relatives after converting to Christianity. Despite this brutal attack, the family's faith remains strong, and their eldest daughter continues to attend church and share the Gospel with others. This testimony serves as a powerful reminder of the cost of following Jesus and the need for His followers to persevere in the face of persecution. It also highlights the resilience of the Church in India, which continues to grow and thrive despite the challenges it faces.

Kingdom Perspective: Even in the midst of persecution and suffering, the Kingdom of God advances, as believers remain faithful to Jesus and His lordship, even in the face of death, and are transformed by His presence into a people who are salt and light in the world.

Kingdom Living

Kingdom Living: Advancing God's Kingdom in Every Sphere

This month, as we focus on Kingdom Advancement, we're reminded that our daily lives are a crucial front in the battle to see the Kingdom of God expand. As Kingdom citizens, we're called to live out our faith in every sphere of society, from our homes to our workplaces, communities, and relationships.

Scripture reminds us, "Whatever you do, do your work heartily, as for the Lord rather than for men" (Colossians 3:23). In light of this, here are 4 practical action steps to help you advance the Kingdom in your daily life:

  1. Claim your workplace as a Kingdom territory: As you go about your daily tasks, pray over your workspace, asking God to fill it with His presence and wisdom. Identify opportunities to serve others and reflect Jesus' love in your interactions.
  2. Create a Kingdom culture at home: Set aside time to pray with your family, discussing how you can live out your faith together. Encourage one another to serve others, volunteer in your community, and engage in Gospel conversations.
  3. Engage your community with Kingdom generosity: Look for opportunities to serve your neighbors, whether it's through volunteering at a local food bank, hosting a block party, or simply offering to help a neighbor with a task.
  4. Seek out Kingdom relationships: Invest in friendships that encourage and challenge you to live out your faith. Look for opportunities to share your faith with others, and be willing to listen to and learn from their perspectives.

This month, let's commit to living out our faith in every sphere of society, trusting that as we do, the Kingdom of God will advance in powerful ways.

Scripture of the Month

Matthew 25:31-33 (ESV)

"When the Son of Man comes in his glory, and all the angels with him, then he will sit on his glorious throne. Before him will be gathered all the nations, and he will separate people one from another as a shepherd separates the sheep from the goats. And he will place the sheep on his right, but the goats on the left. Then the King will say to those on his right, 'Come, you who are blessed by my Father, inherit the kingdom prepared for you from the foundation of the world.'"

As we reflect on Kingdom Advancement, we see Jesus as the King who will one day gather all nations before Him. This passage reveals the present reality of His Kingdom, even as we wait for His return. The King's judgment will separate the sheep from the goats, highlighting the distinction between those who have lived in obedience to His rule and those who have not. Those who have demonstrated love and care for the vulnerable will be welcomed into the Kingdom, while those who have prioritized their own interests will be rejected.

This passage encourages us to see our daily lives as opportunities to advance the Kingdom. How can we love our neighbors, care for the poor, and live in obedience to Jesus' rule in our workplaces, communities, and families? By living out the values of the Kingdom, we bring light and hope to a world in need.

Meditation: How will you prioritize loving and serving those in need this month, as a demonstration of your allegiance to Jesus' Kingdom rule?
